# Research Rubric

Use this file when the prompt is vague, politically sensitive, or broad enough that ranking choices matter.

## Intake Template

Capture these fields from the prompt:
- topic or issue area
- geography
- desired number of names
- media type: print, digital, broadcast, radio, newsletters, or mixed
- outlet tier: top-tier, beltway, trade, local, regional, or mixed
- exclusions or must-have outlets

If the number of names is missing, ask for it before building the list.

## Ranking Factors

Rank names using this order of importance:
1. direct beat fit
2. relevant coverage in the last 6 to 12 months
3. outlet fit for the user's ask
4. geography fit
5. evidence of sustained coverage, not just one-off mentions

## Recommendation Notes

For each proposed reporter, be ready to explain:
- why they fit the prompt
- what they cover
- what outlet they are at now
- one to three recent or highly relevant pieces

## Confirmation Format

Before contact enrichment, present a concise approval-ready list that includes:
- full name
- outlet
- title
- topics covered
- a few relevant links

Invite the user to approve, remove, swap, or expand names.

## Quality Traps

Watch for:
- stale outlet changes
- editors who no longer report
- newsletter authors presented as beat reporters without justification
- general assignment reporters with weak subject fit
- names copied from old lists without current verification
